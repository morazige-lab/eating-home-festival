Curatorial Callout

Not only nostalgia — food also reveals conflict, migration and the negotiation of belonging.

This festival does not treat food as decoration or warm sentiment alone. Through meals, kitchens and rituals, cinema shows tension between generations, cultural translation across borders, and the question of who is included at the table.

Conflict at the Table

In Eat Drink Man Woman, food is loving and beautiful, but it also exposes silence, authority and intergenerational tension.

Inherited Memory

The Joy Luck Club shows how food and family ritual carry memory across generations, while also revealing cultural distance and translation.

Belonging as Performance

Crazy Rich Asians turns food into spectacle: street food, banquets and dumpling-making become tests of class, culture and acceptance.

Migration and Rebuilding Home

Minari shifts the theme from memory to survival. Food, planting and land become ways of rebuilding home in an unfamiliar place.

Taste as Return

Ratatouille offers a comparative Hollywood perspective: taste becomes an emotional doorway back to childhood and a feeling of home.
